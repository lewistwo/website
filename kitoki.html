'kitoki inn' is a Japanese inspired bathhouse and spa, lovingly constructed in and amongst the forests of Bowen Island, British Columbia. It's a place to unwind and foster a renewed connection between oneself and the natural world.

I was tasked with creating the brand identity and building out a visual experience that conveys these themes to attract guests into the world of ‘kitoki inn’.

Image by Ola-Iacovlenco

The wordmark is a collage of English interpretations, meaning 'tree and spirit'. The symbols that stay beneath the logo lockup are traditional Kanji symbols.

Bowen Island, B.C. Canada.

The idea of connecting with nature is at the heart kitoki inn. The term was coined in Japan in the 1980s as a response to the growing disconnection from nature in urbanized lifestyles. In the iconography, we drew upon the linear shapes of trees to create a bold and recognisable representation of nature and the forest.
