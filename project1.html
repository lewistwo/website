Kitoki Inn

An ancient Japanese tradition in harmony with nature

Kitoki Inn offers a Japanese-inspired experience in the stunning surroundings of Bowen Island, British Columbia. The term 'kitoki' reflects themes of folklore, translating to 'tree and spirit,' fostering a renewed connection between oneself and the natural world. This retreat follows the traditional Japanese guest house style, nestled amidst woodlands and forest walks. Embracing the concept of 'bathing in nature,' Kitoki Inn provides bathhouse and spa facilities to rejuvenate and restore the mind and body.

The logo mark combines the English interpretation of 'tree and spirit' with traditional Kanji symbols, using the symbol of trees to create a bold and simple representation of nature. The typography is crafted to guide the eye smoothly through page layouts, incorporating italics and mostly lowercase letterforms to subtly integrate design elements. Throughout the design process, we worked closely with the resort owners to create an authentic storytelling and design approach, capturing the essence of their offering. We blended ancient Japanese minimalist aesthetics with a hand-drawn 'wabi-sabi' style, celebrating the connection between humans and nature. To prioritize sustainability, the identity exclusively utilizes organic and sustainable paper stock, crafted from rapidly renewable hemp and straw fibers, as well as recycled cotton textiles. We consciously opted for a single color in printed pieces. The art direction embraces an unkept, imperfect, and spontaneous style, while maintaining a calm and understated feel. It draws heavily from the architectural features of the cabin designs and celebrates their connection to nature. The guest booklet showcases a montage of textural photographic studies of nature scattered loosely across the pages, highlighting the harmony of the surroundings that guests can experience on Bowen Island.
